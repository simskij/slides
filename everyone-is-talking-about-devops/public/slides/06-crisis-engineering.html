If you're not doing chaos engineering,
you're embracing crisis engineering.
According to Jim Scheibmeir, Senior Principal Analyst, Gartner: “The reality is that avoiding chaos engineering is equivalent to embracing crisis engineering.” And I really think he hits the nail on the head. If you systematically and empirically try to expose weaknesses in your systems, your infrastructure, or your architecture, you'll obviously be a lot better equipped to deal with them before a real incident even occurs.
